About

Do you have a hard time finding accurate, reliable, and honest sources on a topic you know little about? Finding good content is difficult, especially when you’re setting out to learn something new. Unacademic is an open-source platform for collecting and sharing learning resources.

We believe that learning is not a skill but an ethos: a set of shared values. In our vision, learning is inherently social, because you learn best when collaborating with others. Learning is a process of constant negotiation with content and with people.

Search Unacademic for people you want to learn from, or find your own articles, videos and podcasts on a topic. Add them to an existing list or create your own. You can learn - or teach - what you want, when you want, where you want surrounded by likeminded peers. Learn by dwelling.

Curate the Web

How many wheels have you had to reinvent already? Our guess would be ‘too many’. Somehow, we keep creating new textbooks and courses, but most information is out there already. You just need a set up that organizes the information in one place, with just the right amount of structure. You don’t want to be told what to learn, but you don’t want to have to figure out everything yourself either. So gather those interesting blog posts, podcasts, or tutorials, add your comments, and share them. That’s it!

Switch Modes

Learn more effectively by switching between three modes – learning, curating, and browsing. Be on the constant lookout for the clearest best resources for you learning needs. Changing your perspective makes your learning more active, and your retention will surge. Because you’re switching from the big picture to the small picture and back again, your understanding of a topic –from starting your own vineyard, to creating an awesome platform game– will be impressive.

Distribute Versions

Say, you decide to work through someone’s curated list on Unacademic. You start by making a copy of that list. This list is now entirely yours: make your own additions or changes. Or say you are the one who curated the original list. When someone makes changes to a copy, you get a notification. You can choose to accept the changes and include them in your list, or ignore them. The content stays on the move, and so do you. If many people have engaged with a list, you know that the content is compelling and up to date. If many people engage with your list, you’ll become an authority.

Track Progress

Sharing and comparing your progress with others is a proven way to keep you motivated. But if you’re not competitive, it’s still good to know that you’re not alone. Unacademic allows you to track your progress and see how you measure up against your peers. Whether you’re in it for the simple joy of learning or to build your presence in a community, it will help you stay motivated and stimulate you to reach just a little bit further.
